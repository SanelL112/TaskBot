### **A. Translation Lexicon (The "Rosetta Stone")**
*Source: `1_Basics.pdf` Exercises 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6; Practice Test 1, 3, 8*

| English Phrase | Algebraic Operation | **SAT Trap / Nuance** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **"Twice the product of $m$ and $n$"** | $2(mn)$ or $2mn$ | Not $2m \cdot n$ (ambiguous). Parentheses matter. |
| **"Decreased by"** | **Subtraction** (First - Second) | "A decreased by B" = $A - B$. **Reverse order!** |
| **"Less than"** | **Subtraction** (Second - First) | "3 less than $x$" = $x - 3$. **Major SAT Trap.** |
| **"The square of the sum"** | $(m+n)^2$ | **$\neq m^2 + n^2$**. Expansion: $m^2 + 2mn + n^2$. |
| **"The sum of the squares"** | $m^2 + n^2$ | Distinct from above. |
| **"Quotient of $A$ and $B$"** | $\frac{A}{B}$ or $A \div B$ | Order matters: $A$ is numerator. |
| **"Increased by"** | Addition | "The quotient... increased by 7" = $\frac{19}{d} + 7$. |
| **"$k$ times the sum of..."** | $k(\dots)$ | Distribute immediately. |
| **"Halfway between $a$ and $b$"** | $\frac{a+b}{2}$ | Average formula. Works on number line (Practice Test Q4). |

### **B. Order of Operations (PEMDAS/GEMDAS) - Non-Negotiable**
*Source: `1_Basics.pdf` Exponents/Order of Ops Exercises 1–8; Practice Test Q10, 12*

1.  **G**rouping Symbols: `( )`, `[ ]`, `{ }`, **Fraction Bars**, **Radical Bars**, Absolute Value `| |`.
2.  **E**xponents & Radicals.
3.  **M**ultiplication & **D**ivision (Left $\to$ Right).
4.  **A**ddition & **S**ubtraction (Left $\to$ Right).

**SAT Specifics:**
*   **Fraction Bars imply Parentheses:** $\frac{2(7-9)}{8}$ means $(2(7-9)) \div 8$.
*   **Negative Signs & Exponents:** $-3^2 = -9$ vs $(-3)^2 = 9$. *The SAT tests this constantly.*
*   **Complex Fractions:** Simplify top and bottom separately *before* dividing.

### **C. Simplifying Expressions: The "Structural" Rules**
*Source: `1_Basics.pdf` Simplifying Exercises 1–6;56; Practice Test 6, 9*

1.  **Distributive Property:** $a(b+c) = ab + ac$. **Watch for negative signs:** $-2(x-3) = -2x + 6$.
2.  **Combining Like Terms:** Match **variable** AND **exponent** exactly. $x^2 \neq x$.
3.  **Nested Grouping:** Work **Inside $\to$ Out**.
    *   Ex: $3[6 - 3(1-a) - 5(a-1)]$ $\to$ Simplify inside brackets first.
4.  **Rational Expression Simplification:**
    *   Factor numerator/denominator $\to$ Cancel **factors** (not terms!).
    *   $\frac{a^2 - a - 6}{a-3} = \frac{(a-3)(a+2)}{a-3} = a+2 \quad (a \neq 3)$.

### **D. Number Classification & Properties**
*Source: `1_Basics.pdf` Rational/Irrational Exercises 16; Practice Test 4, 5*

| Set | Definition | SAT Examples |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Integers** | $\{\dots, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, \dots\}$ | Includes 0 and Negatives! |
| **Rational** | Can be written as $\frac{p}{q}$ ($q \neq 0$). Terminating or Repeating decimals. | $-1.2, \frac{3}{4}, \sqrt{\frac{49}{64}} = \frac{7}{8}, 0.\overline{9}=1$ |
| **Irrational** | Non-terminating, Non-repeating decimals. Roots of non-perfect squares. | $\sqrt{3}, \sqrt{10}, \pi, e$ |
| **Real** | Rational $\cup$ Irrational. | Everything on the number line. |

**Key SAT Fact:** $\sqrt{x^2} = |x|$ (Not $x$). The principal root is non-negative.

### **E. FACTORING TOOLBOX (The Kuta Worksheet Core)**
*Source: `Kuta Worksheet` Problems 13–20. **This is Passport to Advanced Math.***

#### **1. GCF (Greatest Common Factor) — ALWAYS STEP 1**
*   **Rule:** Factor out the largest coefficient and lowest exponent of shared variables.
*   **Ex 15:** $84v^3 + 98v^2 - 60v - 70$
    *   GCF = $2$ $\to$ $2(42v^3 + 49v^2 - 30v - 35)$ $\to$ Then Group.

#### **2. Factor by Grouping (4 Terms)**
*   **Pattern:** $ax^3 + bx^2 + cx + d$ (or 4 terms).
*   **Algorithm:**
    1.  Group pairs: $(ax^3 + bx^2) + (cx + d)$.
    2.  Factor GCF from *each* group.
    3.  **Match the Binomial:** $( \dots )( \text{Common Binomial} )$.
*   **Ex 13:** $5k^3 - 15k^2 - 4k + 12$
    *   Group: $(5k^3 - 15k^2) + (-4k + 12)$
    *   GCFs: $5k^2(k - 3) - 4(k - 3)$
    *   Result: **$(k - 3)(5k^2 - 4)$**

#### **3. Difference of Squares (2 Terms, Subtraction, Perfect Squares)**
*   **Formula:** $a^2 - b^2 = (a - b)(a + b)$
*   **Ex 17:** $25x^2 - 16 = (5x)^2 - 4^2 = \mathbf{(5x - 4)(5x + 4)}$
*   **Ex 18:** $4x^2 - 25 = (2x)^2 - 5^2 = \mathbf{(2x - 5)(2x + 5)}$
*   **SAT Twist:** $x^4 - 16 = (x^2 - 4)(x^2 + 4) = (x-2)(x+2)(x^2+4)$. **Factor completely!**

#### **4. Sum / Difference of Cubes (2 Terms, Cubes)**
*   **Memorize SOAP:** **S**ame, **O**pposite, **A**lways **P**ositive.
*   **Difference:** $a^3 - b^3 = (a - b)(a^2 + ab + b^2)$
*   **Sum:** $a^3 + b^3 = (a + b)(a^2 - ab + b^2)$
*   **Ex 19:** $8x^3 - 125 = (2x)^3 - 5^3 = \mathbf{(2x - 5)(4x^2 + 10x + 25)}$
*   **Ex 20:** $64x^3 + 1 = (4x)^3 + 1^3 = \mathbf{(4x + 1)(16x^2 - 4x + 1)}$
*   **Note:** The trinomial factor **NEVER factors further** over Reals.

## 🛠️ 3. STEP-BY-STEP TUTORIALS: SAT WORKFLOWS

### **Workflow A: The "Word Problem $\to$ Expression" Pipeline**
*Target: `1_Basics.pdf` Practice Test Q1, Q3, Q8*

> **Problem:** *"Two less than the quotient of three and a number $n$."*
> **Step 1: Identify the "Main Verb" (The last operation).** "Is" / "Equals" / "Result is" $\to$ Usually the final output. Here, the phrase *is* the expression.
> **Step 2: Parse "Quotient of 3 and n".** $\to \frac{3}{n}$.
> **Step 3: Parse "Two less than [Result]".** $\to$ **Subtract 2 from the result.** $\frac{3}{n} - 2$.
> **Step 4: Match Format.** SAT loves $\frac{3-2n}{n}$ (common denominator). $\frac{3}{n} - \frac{2n}{n} = \frac{3-2n}{n}$.
> **Answer:** $\frac{3-2n}{n}$ (Matches Choice C/D format usually).

> **Problem (PT Q3):** *"Johnny received $m$ texts Fri... 3 less than twice Sat... 5 more Sun..."*
> **Step 1: Define Variables for each day.**
> Fri $= m$
> Sat $= 2m - 3$ ("3 less than twice Fri")
> Sun $= (2m - 3) + 5 = 2m + 2$ ("5 more than Sat")
> **Step 2: Total = Sum.** $m + (2m - 3) + (2m + 2) = 5m - 1$.
> **Step 3: Scan Choices.** $5m - 1$. (Choice B: $5m-1$? Wait, choices were $4m+2, 5m-1, 4m-2, 5m+1$. Correct is **$5m-1$**).

### **Workflow B: The "Order of Ops / Evaluation" Pipeline**
*Target: `1_Basics.pdf` Exponents Ex 5, 6, 7, 8; PT Q10, Q12*

> **Problem (Ex 5):** Evaluate $2(c+a)(b-a) \times 10$ if $a=-2, b=3, c=5$.
> **Step 1: SUBSTITUTE WITH PARENTHESES.** $2(5 + (-2))(3 - (-2)) \times 10$.
> **Step 2: SIMPLIFY INSIDE PARENS.** $2(3)(5) \times 10$.
> **Step 3: MULTIPLY LEFT TO RIGHT.** $6 \times 5 \times 10 = 30 \times 10 = 300$.
> **SAT Hack:** If answer choices are numbers, **Plug & Chug**. If variables, simplify algebraically first.

> **Problem (PT Q12):** $ \frac{1}{2}(2a - 7) \div 3b - c $ ... wait, PDF says: `12 (2 )73ab c−÷−−+`? (OCR Garbled).

> **Rule:** Write substitution clearly. $\frac{1}{2}(2(3) - 7) \div (3(-1) - (-2))$.
> **Numerator:** $\frac{1}{2}(6-7) = \frac{1}{2}(-1) = -0.5$.
> **Denominator:** $-3 + 2 = -1$.
> **Result:** $(-0.5) / (-1) = 0.5$.

### **4. Answer: D**

### 2.2 Exponent & Radical Rules (Non-Negotiable)
*   **Product:** $x^a \cdot x^b = x^{a+b}$
*   **Quotient:** $x^a / x^b = x^{a-b}$
*   **Power to Power:** $(x^a)^b = x^{ab}$
*   **Negative Exponent:** $x^{-a} = 1/x^a$
*   **Zero Exponent:** $x^0 = 1 \ (x \neq 0)$
*   **Fractional Exponent:** $x^{m/n} = \sqrt[n]{x^m} = (\sqrt[n]{x})^m$
*   **Distributing over Product/Quotient:** $(xy)^a = x^a y^a$ ; $(x/y)^a = x^a / y^a$
*   **⚠️ TRAP:** $(x+y)^2 \neq x^2 + y^2$. It is $x^2 + 2xy + y^2$.

### 2.3 Factoring Toolbox (Kuta Worksheet Mastery)
**Always check GCF FIRST.**
1.  **Difference of Squares:** $a^2 - b^2 = (a-b)(a+b)$
    *   *Ex: $25x^2 - 16 = (5x-4)(5x+4)$*
2.  **Perfect Square Trinomials:** $a^2 \pm 2ab + b^2 = (a \pm b)^2$
3.  **Sum/Difference of Cubes:**
    *   $a^3 + b^3 = (a+b)(a^2 - ab + b^2)$  *(SOAP: Same, Opposite, Always Positive)*
    *   $a^3 - b^3 = (a-b)(a^2 + ab + b^2)$
    *   *Ex: $8x^3 - 125 = (2x)^3 - 5^3 = (2x-5)(4x^2+10x+25)$*
    *   *Ex: $64x^3 + 1 = (4x)^3 + 1^3 = (4x+1)(16x^2 - 4x + 1)$*
4.  **Factoring by Grouping (4 Terms):**
    *   Group pairs $\rightarrow$ Factor GCF from each $\rightarrow$ Factor common binomial.
    *   *Ex: $5k^3 - 15k^2 - 4k + 12 = 5k^2(k-3) - 4(k-3) = (k-3)(5k^2-4)$*

### 2.4 Linear & Quadratic Forms (Desmos Ready)
| Form | Equation | Key Features (Desmos Input) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Slope-Intercept** | $y = mx + b$ | `y = mx + b` (m=slope, b=y-int) |

## 2️⃣ **CORE CONCEPTS & FORMULAS**

### 📐 **MATH: HEART OF ALGEBRA & ADVANCED MATH**

#### **Linear Functions & Systems**
| Form | Equation | When to Use |
|------|----------|-------------|
| **Slope-Intercept** | $y = mx + b$ | Graphing, identifying slope ($m$) & $y$-intercept ($b$) |
| **Point-Slope** | $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$ | Modeling from a point & slope |
| **Standard** | $Ax + By = C$ | Word problems, systems, intercepts ($A,B,C$ integers) |

**Slope Formula**:  
$$m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}$$

**Interpretation**: *"The dependent variable changes by $m$ units for every 1 unit increase in the independent variable."*

**Systems of Equations**:
- **One solution**: Lines intersect ($m_1 \ne m_2$) → **Consistent & Independent**
- **No solution**: Parallel lines ($m_1 = m_2, b_1 \ne b_2$) → **Inconsistent**
- **Infinite solutions**: Same line ($m_1 = m_2, b_1 = b_2$) → **Dependent**

**Solving Methods**:
1. **Substitution** (when a variable has coefficient $\pm 1$)
2. **Elimination** (scale to match coefficients, add/subtract)
3. **Graphing/Desmos** (visual confirmation)

#### **Quadratics: Three Forms & Key Features**
| Form | Equation | Vertex | Roots | Axis of Symmetry |
|------|----------|--------|-------|------------------|
| **Standard** | $y = ax^2 + bx + c$ | $\left(-\frac{b}{2a},\ f\left(-\frac{b}{2a}\right)\right)$ | Quadratic Formula | $x = -\frac{b}{2a}$ |
| **Vertex** | $y = a(x-h)^2 + k$ | $(h, k)$ | Square Root Method | $x = h$ |
| **Intercept** | $y = a(x-p)(x-q)$ | $\left(\frac{p+q}{2},\ f\left(\frac{p+q}{2}\right)\right)$ | $x = p, q$ | $x = \frac{p+q}{2}$ |

**Quadratic Formula**:  
$$x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$$

**Discriminant** ($\Delta = b^2 - 4ac$):
- $\Delta > 0$ & perfect square → 2 rational roots
- $\Delta > 0$ & not perfect square → 2 irrational roots
- $\Delta = 0$ → 1 real root (vertex on $x$-axis)
- $\Delta < 0$ → No real roots

**Factoring Patterns (AHA Academy Emphasis)**:
- **Difference of Squares**: $a^2 - b^2 = (a+b)(a-b)$
- **Perfect Square Trinomial**: $a^2 \pm 2ab + b^2 = (a \pm b)^2$
- **AC Method: **AC Method** (for $ax^2+bx+c$, $a \ne 1$): Find factors of $ac$ summing to $b$, split middle term, group.

#### **Exponential & Polynomial Modeling**
- **Exponential Growth/Decay**: $y = a(1 \pm r)^t$ or $y = ab^t$
- **Compound Interest**: $A = P\left(1 + \frac{r}{n}\right)^{nt}$
- **Factor Theorem**: $f(k)=0 \iff (x-k)$ is a factor

#### **Geometry & Trigonometry (SAT Focus)**
**Special Right Triangles**:
| Triangle | Angles | Side Ratios | Area |
|----------|--------|-------------|------|
| **45-45-90** | $45^\circ, 45^\circ, 90^\circ$ | $s : s : s\sqrt{2}$ | $\frac{s^2}{2}$ |
| **30-60-90** | $30^\circ, 60^\circ, 90^\circ$ | $s : s\sqrt{3} : 2s$ | $\frac{s^2\sqrt{3}}{2}$ |

**Pythagorean Triples**: $(3,4,5),\ (5,12,13),\ (8,15,17),\ (7,24,25)$ and multiples.

**Trigonometric Ratios (SOH-CAH-TOA)**:
$$\sin \theta = \frac{\text{opp}}{\text{hyp}},\quad \cos \theta = \frac{\text{adj}}{\text{hyp}},\quad \tan \theta = \frac{\text{opp}}{\text{adj}}$$

**Cofunction Identity**:  
$$\sin \theta = \cos(90^\circ - \theta) \quad \text{for acute } \theta$$

**Triangle Area**:
- $\frac{1}{2}bh$
- $\frac{1}{2}ab\sin C$ (SAS)
- Heron's: $\sqrt{s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)}$, $s = \frac{a+b+c}{2}$

**Similar Triangles**: $\triangle ABC \sim \triangle XYZ \iff \angle A \cong \angle X,\ \angle B \cong \angle Y,\ \angle C \cong \angle Z$ AND $\frac{AB}{XY} = \frac{BC}{YZ} = \frac{AC}{XZ}$

**Circle Equations**: $(x-h)^2 + (y-k)^2 = r^2$

### 📝 **READING & WRITING: GRAMMAR & RHETORIC**

#### **Punctuation Hierarchy (AHA Academy Day 3)**
| Mark | Primary Use | SAT Trap |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Comma** | Lists, ICC + IC, ECC, MCC (nonessential), coordinate adjectives | Comma splice (IC, IC), false FANBOYS (however, therefore) |
| **Semicolon** | IC ; IC (no conjunction), complex lists | Never with FANBOYS |
| **Colon** | IC : list/explanation (IC must precede) | After "such as", "including", "for example" |
| **Dash** | IC — emphasis/clarification (often surprising) | Pair for MCC (— ... —) |
| **Apostrophe** | Contractions (it's = it is), possession | No apostrophe for possessive pronouns (its, theirs) |

**Clause Types**:
- **ICC** (Introductory Comma Clause): Starts with subordinating conj/prep/ing-word → **must** be followed by comma + logical subject.
- **ECC** (Ending Comma Clause): Usually starts with -ing adverb → defines/clarifies.
- **MCC** (Middle Comma Clause): Appositive/nonessential → set off by **two** commas/dashes/parentheses.
- **Relative Clause**: Begins with *who, whom, whose, which, that* → no comma before *that*.
- **Prepositional Phrase**: Preposition → noun (object) → **never** contains subject/verb.

#### **Transition Taxonomy (AHA Academy Day 4)**
| Category | Transitions | Relationship | SAT Signal |
|----------|-------------|--------------|------------|
| **Contrast** | however, on the other hand, conversely, whereas, yet | Opposing view / balance | $\text{Claim}_1 \rightarrow \text{Oppose/Balance}$ |
| **Evidence** | in fact, indeed, for example, for instance | Support claim | $\text{Claim} \rightarrow \text{Specific Evidence}$ |
| **Addition** | furthermore, moreover, additionally, also | New supporting evidence | $\text{Evidence}_1 \rightarrow \text{Evidence}_2$ |
| **Cause/Effect** | therefore, consequently, thus, because | Causal link | $\text{Cause} \rightarrow \text{Effect}$ |
| **Concession** | granted, nevertheless, although, even if | Concede → stronger claim | $\text{Weaker} \rightarrow \text{Stronger}$ |
| **Sequence** | subsequently, previously, next, finally | Time/order | $\text{Event}_1 \rightarrow \text{Event}_2$ |
| **Similarity** | similarly, likewise, in the same way | Shared theme | $\text{Claim}_1 \rightarrow \text{Claim}_2 (\text{similar})$ |
| **Conclusion** | in conclusion, in short, ultimately | Restate/summarize | $\text{Idea} \rightarrow \text{Summary}$ |

**Process**: ① Read pre-transition clause → determine function. ② Read post-transition clause → identify relationship. ③ Match transition to relationship.

#### **Subject-Verb Agreement (AHA Day 2)**
1. **Ignore** prepositional phrases, relative clauses, commas clauses.
2. **Find** true subject (never in a phrase/clause; before verb unless "there is/are" or question).
3. **Singular subjects** → singular verbs (usually end in -s); **Plural** → plural verbs (no -s).
4. **Compound subjects** (with *and*) → plural. *Each/Every* → singular. *Number* → *a number* (plural), *the number* (singular).

#### **Verb Tense Consistency (College Panda Ch. 15)**
| Context | Tense | Keywords |
|---------|-------|----------|
| General fact / habitual | Simple Present | always, every day, generally |
| Completed past action | Simple Past | yesterday, in 2010, last week |
| Action started past → continues present | Present Perfect | since, for, recently, already |
| Past action before another past | Past Perfect | before, by the time, already |
| Future from past perspective | Conditional (would) | said he would, planned to |

### **QUESTION 1** (Heart of Algebra — System Classification)
The system of equations below has **no solution**. What is the value of $k$?
$$
\begin{cases}
2x + 5y = 8 \\
4x + ky = 17
\end{cases}
$$
**A)** $10$  
**B)** $8$  
**C)** $5$  
**D)** $2.5$

<details>
<summary>🔍 <strong>Solution</strong></summary>

**No solution** $\iff$ lines are parallel $\iff$ same slope, different intercepts.  
Rewrite in slope-intercept:  
$y = -\frac{2}{5}x + \frac{8}{5}$ and $y = -\frac{4}{k}x + \frac{17}{k}$.  
Set slopes equal: $-\frac{2}{5} = -\frac{4}{k} \implies 2k = 20 \implies k = 10$.  
Check intercepts: $\frac{8}{5} \ne \frac{17}{10}$ ✓.  
**Answer: A**
</details>

### **QUESTION 2** (Quadratics — Vertex Form & Interpretation)
A projectile's height (meters) is modeled by $h(t) = -5(t-3)^2 + 45$, where $t$ is seconds after launch.  
Which statement is **true**?
**A)** The projectile reaches maximum height at $t = 5$ seconds.  
**B)** The maximum height is $45$ meters.  
**C)** The projectile hits the ground at $t = 3$ seconds.  
**D)** The initial height is $45$ meters.

<details>
<summary>🔍 <strong>Solution</strong></summary>

Vertex form: $h(t) = a(t-h)^2 + k$ → vertex $(h, k) = (3, 45)$.  
Since $a = -5 < 0$, parabola opens down → **vertex = maximum**.  
- Max height $45$ m at $t = 3$ s → **B is correct**.  
- A: wrong time. C: hits ground when $h=0 \Rightarrow t=0$ or $6$. D: initial height $h(0) = -5(9)+45 = 0$.
</details>

### **QUESTION 3** (Trigonometry — Cofunction Identity)
In right triangle $ABC$, right angle at $C$. If $\sin A = \frac{12}{13}$, what is $\cos B$?
**A)** $\frac{5}{13}$  
**B)** $\frac{12}{13}$  
**C)** $\frac{13}{12}$  
**D)** $\frac{5}{12}$

<details>
<summary>🔍 <strong>Solution</strong></summary>

In a right triangle, acute angles are complementary: $A + B = 90^\circ$.  
Cofunction identity: $\sin A = \cos(90^\circ - A) = \cos B$.  
Thus $\cos B = \sin A = \frac{12}{13}$.  
**Answer: B**  
*(Note: $\cos A = \frac{5}{13}$ from 5-12-13 triangle, but question asks for $\cos B$.)*
</details>

### **QUESTION 4** (Problem Solving — Rates & Linear Modeling) 🖩
A 500-gallon tank is being filled by two pipes. Pipe A delivers $12$ gal/min; Pipe B delivers $8$ gal/min. Pipe B starts **10 minutes after** Pipe A. Which equation gives the time $t$ (minutes after Pipe A starts) when the tank is full?
**A)** $12t + 8(t-10) = 500$  
**B)** $12t + 8t = 500$  
**C)** $12(t+10) + 8t = 500$  
**D)** $20t = 500$

<details>
<summary>🔍 <strong>Solution</strong></summary>

Let $t$ = minutes since Pipe A started.  
- Pipe A runs $t$ minutes → delivers $12t$ gallons.  
- Pipe B starts at $t=10$, so runs $(t-10)$ minutes → delivers $8(t-10-10)$ gallons.  
Total volume: $12t + 8(t-10) = 500$.  
**Answer: A**  
*(Solve: $20t - 80 = 500 \Rightarrow 20t = 580 \Rightarrow t = 29$ min.)*
</details>

### **QUESTION 5** (Advanced Math — Polynomial Factors & Graph)
The polynomial $p(x) = x^3 - 6x^2 + 11x - 6$ has three real zeros. What is the **sum of the $x$-coordinates** of the points where the graph of $y = p(x)$ crosses the $x$-axis?
**A)** $6$  
**B)** $11$  
**C)** $-6$  
**D)** $0$

<details>
<summary>🔍 <strong>Solution</strong></summary>

Zeros = $x$-intercepts. For cubic $x^3 + bx^2 + cx + d$, sum of roots $= -b$ (Vieta's formulas).  
Here $b = -6$, so sum $= -(-6) = 6$.  
**Verify**: $p(x) = (x-1)(x-2)(x-3)$ → roots $1,2,3$ → sum $= 6$.  
**Answer: A**  
*(Shortcut: On SAT, sum of roots of $ax^n + bx^{n-1} + \dots = -b/a$.)*
</details>
