### 2. SAT Mathematics (Source: [pdf1.txt](file:///home/sanel/.gemini/antigravity-cli/scratch/pdf1.txt), [pdf2.txt](file:///home/sanel/.gemini/antigravity-cli/scratch/pdf2.txt), [pdf3.txt](file:///home/sanel/.gemini/antigravity-cli/scratch/pdf3.txt))
*   **Percents & Word Problems**:
    *   *Comparisons & Standards*: The "standard" is always the second item listed in a comparison. Percents are multiplied by the original/whole/standard value.
    *   *Percent Change*: Calculated as $\frac{\text{change}}{\text{old/standard}} \times 100$.
    *   *Ages over Time*: Understanding that percent difference in ages decreases over time as both individuals age.
    *   *Category Addition*: Add percentages only when they represent mutually exclusive categories of a single whole.
*   **Mixture Problems**:
    *   *Methodology*: Track active ingredient mass/volume separate from the total mixture volume.
    *   *Equation Structure*: $\text{Volume}_1 \cdot \text{Percent}_1 + \text{Volume}_2 \cdot \text{Percent}_2 = \text{Total Volume} \cdot \text{Target Percent}$. (e.g., adding $x$ liters of 25% acid solution to 40L of 40% acid solution to make a 30% acidic solution).
*   **Factor of Change**:
    *   *Methodology*: Write the formula, eliminate all constants, replace variables with their factor of change, solve for the unknown factor of change, and calculate the percent increase/decrease relative to 1. (e.g., if mass decreases by 20% and speed increases by 20%, kinetic energy $K = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$ changes by a factor of $0.8 \times 1.2^2 = 1.152$, showing a 15.2% increase).
*   **Ratios & Proportions**:
    *   *Methodology*: Ratios represent simplified values and do not provide exact quantities. Each part should be assigned a multiple variable (e.g., boys:girls = 2:3 yields $2m$ and $3m$).
    *   *Rectangle Area & Perimeter*: Using algebraic proportions to solve dimensions (e.g., width to length ratio of 1:2 with an area of 200 leads to width = 10, length = 20, and perimeter = 60).
*   **Rates, Speed & Average Speed**:
    *   *Methodology*: $\text{Quantity} = \text{Rate} \cdot \text{Consumption}$. Average speed is $\frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Total Time}}$.
    *   *Multi-Speed Problems*: Construct a distance-speed-time chart for each leg of the trip. (e.g., driving to a destination at 40 mph and returning at 60 mph yields an average trip speed of 48 mph).
*   **Trigonometry**:
    *   *Core Ratios*: Sine ($\sin = \text{opp/hyp}$), Cosine ($\cos = \text{adj/hyp}$), Tangent ($\tan = \text{opp/adj}$), and their reciprocals: Cosecant ($\csc$), Secant ($\sec$), Cotangent ($\cot$).
    *   *Complementary Angles (Co-functions)*: $\sin(A) = \cos(B)$ when $A + B = 90^\circ$ (e.g., $\sin(37^\circ) = \cos(53^\circ) = 3/5$, or $\tan(A) = 4/7$).
    *   *Problem-Solving*: Right triangles are required. For missing angles, label all sides in relation to the target angle and apply inverse trig functions. For missing sides, set up algebraic equations using standard ratios.
*   **Triangle Geometry**:
    *   *Types*: Isosceles (congruent legs and base angles) and Equilateral (all sides congruent, all angles measuring $60^\circ$).
    *   *Area Formulas*:
        *   Standard: $\frac{1}{2}bh$
        *   Equilateral: $\frac{s^2\sqrt{3}}{4}$
        *   SAS Area: $\frac{1}{2}ab\sin\theta$
        *   Heron's Formula: $\sqrt{s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)}$ where $s = \frac{a+b+c}{2}$ (semiperimeter).
*   **Similarity & Congruence**:
    *   *Similarity*: Postulates (AA, SSS, SAS). Corresponding angles are congruent, and sides are proportional.
    *   *Congruence*: Postulates (SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS, HL).
    *   *Special Similar Triangles*: Back-to-back parallel lines, intermeshed parallel lines, and triple right triangle configurations (altitude $c$ dividing hypotenuse into segments $a$ and $b$, yielding $\frac{a}{c} = \frac{c}{b}$).

### 3. SAT Reading & Grammar (Source: [pdf1.txt](file:///home/sanel/.gemini/antigravity-cli/scratch/pdf1.txt), [pdf2.txt](file:///home/sanel/.gemini/antigravity-cli/scratch/pdf2.txt), [pdf3.txt](file:///home/sanel/.gemini/antigravity-cli/scratch/pdf3.txt))
*   **Verb Tenses**:
    *   Distinguishing simple tenses (present, past, future helpers) and perfect tenses (present perfect, past perfect).
    *   Continuity markers (e.g., *since, until, continuously*) indicate ongoing action and mandate perfect tenses.
*   **Active Reading & Passage Annotation**:
    *   Deep reading requires identifying connections and forecasting events.
    *   *Annotation Process*: Circle/highlight transitional words/phrases (coordinating conjunctions like *but, and, yet*; transitions like *however, though, nevertheless, moreover, although*) and punctuation to foreshadow passage shifts. Verify or adjust these predictions as reading progresses.
*   **Reading Comprehension & Analysis**:
    *   *Contradictions*: Analyze contradictions to identify missed variables.
    *   *Captivity Debate*: Focus on natural vs. controlling environments, ethical considerations, and comparing comparative purposes.
*   **Test-Taking Strategies**:
    *   *Answer Selection*: Draft your own answer using direct passage evidence first. Eliminate "dummy" choices (undiscussed topics, opposing views, or extreme language like *only, always, never*). Check remaining choices word-by-word for single-word errors.
    *   *Evidence Questions*: Highlight the target claim (in text or header), determine what evidence supports it, and evaluate options/graphics against that standard.
*   **Grammar & Punctuation Conventions**:
    *   *Subject-Verb Agreement*: Matching singular/plural subjects with correct verb forms (e.g., *toxins that actually increase attractiveness*, *nematodes that come in contact*).
    *   *Punctuation*: Precise usage of colons (introducing explanations/lists), semicolons (connecting independent clauses), dashes, and parenthetical elements (e.g., appositives like *dendrochronologist Valerie Trouet*).
    *   *Pronouns*: Ensuring correct pronoun-antecedent agreement (e.g., *its* for singular objects like *Orlando Figes's book*, *they* for plural terms).
